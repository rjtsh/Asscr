Building a Healthier, More Sustainable Future

Ensuring good health and well-being for all is essential for fostering vibrant and resilient societies. This goal emphasizes access to quality healthcare, reducing inequalities, and promoting mental health. At the same time, creating sustainable cities and communities focuses on designing urban spaces that are inclusive, safe, and environmentally responsible. By integrating green technologies, improving infrastructure, and enhancing public services, we can ensure cities that support the well-being of current and future generations. These goals are interconnected, aiming to improve both personal and community health while safeguarding our planet for the long term.

Here are six common and legitimate infectious diseases caused by viruses and bacteria that spread particularly among vulnerable populations . Click on a disease to learn more!

Tuberculosis (TB)

Cause: Mycobacterium tuberculosis (bacteria) Spread: Airborne transmission through coughing or sneezing. Vulnerable Groups: People with weakened immune systems, such as those living with HIV, malnutrition, or in overcrowded conditions. TB is especially common in low-income and developing countries.

HIV/AIDS

Cause: Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV) Spread: Through blood, sexual contact, and from mother to child during childbirth or breastfeeding. Vulnerable Groups: Those with poor access to healthcare, individuals engaging in unprotected sex, intravenous drug users, and individuals living in areas with high HIV prevalence.

Pneumonia

Cause: Can be caused by viruses (e.g., influenza, respiratory syncytial virus) or bacteria (e.g., Streptococcus pneumoniae). Spread: Airborne droplets or contact with infected surfaces. Vulnerable Groups: Infants, the elderly, those with compromised immune systems, and individuals with chronic diseases like asthma or heart disease.

Malaria

Cause: Plasmodium species (parasite), transmitted by Anopheles mosquitoes (not a virus or bacteria, but still infectious) Spread: Mosquito bites. Vulnerable Groups: Pregnant women, young children, and people living in tropical and subtropical regions with inadequate healthcare and mosquito control measures.

Hepatitis B and C

Cause: Hepatitis B virus (HBV) and Hepatitis C virus (HCV) Spread: Blood-to-blood contact (e.g., sharing needles, unsterilized medical instruments), sexual contact, or from mother to child. Vulnerable Groups: People with unprotected sex, intravenous drug users, individuals who received unscreened blood transfusions, and those in areas with poor medical infrastructure.

Diphtheria

Cause: Corynebacterium diphtheriae (bacteria) Spread: Through respiratory droplets or contact with infected wounds. Vulnerable Groups: Unvaccinated individuals, especially in regions with poor healthcare infrastructure and limited access to vaccines
